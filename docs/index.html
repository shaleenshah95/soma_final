THE HARMS OF SOLITARY CONFINEMENT

Mental illness in solitary confinement

Behind the walls of U.S. prisons

Shaleen Shah and Chikezie Omeje

Published NOVEMBER 24, 2019

According to a study by The Association of State Correctional Administrators and Yale Law School, one in three prisoners in solitary confinement has a mental illness. There may be a link between solitary confinement and mental illness on a mass, not individual, scale.

Here are the numbers. Out of 8488 prisoners in solitary confinement: One out of three prisoners have mental illness. Among those in suicide watch, seven out of 10 prisoners have mental illness. One percent of prisoners without mental illness are in suicide watch, however, five percent of prisoners with mental illness are in suicide watch.
